The Pigeonhole Principle

CS20 — Interactive Practice
The Pigeonhole Principle: Let f : A → B where A and B are finite sets and |A| > |B|. Then there exist distinct elements x, y ∈ A such that f(x) = f(y). In other words, if you put more pigeons into fewer pigeonholes, at least one hole must contain more than one pigeon.

Problem 5

The Generalized Pigeonhole Principle

(A) Prove the Generalized Pigeonhole Principle by contradiction: if |A| > k·|B|, then some pigeonhole contains more than k pigeons.

(B) There are 366 possible birthdays (including Feb 29). How many people must be in a group to guarantee at least n people share a birthday?
Interactive Visualization
|A| = 10 > 3 × 3 = 9 = k|B|. So some hole has > 3 pigeons.
You need at least 367 people in the group.
Formula: You need (n-1) × 366 + 1 people.
With k = n-1 and |B| = 366, if |A| > k · |B| then some birthday has > k = n-1 pigeons, i.e., at least n.
