Problem: Pythagorean Theorem

Recall The Pythagorean Theorem: in any right triangle with legs a and b and hypotenuse c,  a2 + b2 = c2.


Below are two sets of four identical right triangles and one small square each. How can you use them to construct a visual proof of the theorem? Drag and rotate the shapes to experiment.

Problem guide

  • Consider the idea that area is preserved. Can you make one shape with area c2 and one shape with area a2 + b2?
  • If you are having a hard time making a shape with area a2 + b2, consider the side length of the small square.
